Driving Basics

Step 1:

Complete the online tutorial

Learn the fundamentals of safe driving from the comfort of your own home. Our interactive tutorial covers everything from basic traffic laws to defensive driving techniques.

Step 2:

Pass a driving test

After completing the online tutorial, you'll need to pass a driving test to prove that you've mastered the basics. Our experienced instructors will guide you through the process and help you succeed.

Step 3:

Get on the road

Once you've passed the driving test, you're ready to hit the road! Our flexible scheduling options make it easy to find a time that works for you. Whether you're a busy parent or a student with a packed schedule, we've got you covered.



Earnings

Standard fare

You earn a fare for every completed trip.

Surge

Check the heat map in your app to find out when and where rider demand is high, so you can earn more on top of your standard fare.

Minimum trip earnings

Each city has a minimum amount that you'll earn for any trip. This makes sure your earnings reflect the effort you put in, even on short trips.

Service fee

This fee helps fund things like app development and customer support.

Cancellations

In most instances, you’ll receive a cancellation fee if a rider cancels a request.



Safety

Masks are no longer required

As of April 19, 2022, riders and drivers are not required to wear masks when using Uber. However, the CDC still recommends wearing a mask if you have certain personal risk factors and/or high transmission levels in your area.


Remember: many people still feel safer wearing a mask because of personal or family health situations, so please be respectful of their preferences. And if you ever feel uncomfortable, you can always cancel the trip.

Updating our no-front-seat policy

Riders are no longer required to sit in the back seat. However, to give you more space, we’re still asking that riders only use the front seat if it’s required because of the size of their group.

Thank you for helping take care of one another

We know the pandemic has been difficult. But you’ve continued to go the extra mile to help protect our communities—whether it’s wearing a mask, making space for one another, or helping people get the food they need. Thank you for that.


It’s still important to take safety precautions while driving and delivering. So make sure to roll down the windows for extra airflow, sanitize your hands before and after trips or deliveries, and always cover your cough or sneeze.s.
